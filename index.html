CLASSES OF FOOD

SIX CLASSES OF FOOD

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates, or carbs, are sugar molecules. Along with proteins and fats, carbohydrates are one of three main nutrients found in foods and drinks. Your body breaks down carbohydrates into glucose. Glucose, or blood sugar, is the main source of energy for your body's cells, tissues, and organs.

Carbohydrates (carbs) are a type of macronutrient found in certain foods and drinks. Sugars, starches and fiber are carbohydrates. Other macronutrients include fat and protein. Your body needs balanced macronutrients to stay healthy.

protein

Proteins are made up of building blocks called amino acids. There are about 20 different amino acids that link together in different combinations. Your body uses them to make new proteins, such as muscle and bone, and other compounds such as enzymes and hormones. It can also use them as an energy source.

Vitamins

Vitamins are organic compounds that are essential for human health and growth in small amounts. They are often identified by letters of the alphabet, such as vitamin C, or by their chemical names.

Fats

Fats are a macronutrient in the human diet, along with carbohydrates and proteins. They are a major source of energy for many animals and perform important functions in living beings.

Fiber

Fiber is a carbohydrate found in plants that helps maintain a healthy digestive system. It can also be a material used to make other products.

water

water, a substance composed of the chemical elements hydrogen and oxygen and existing in gaseous, liquid, and solid states. It is one of the most plentiful and essential of compounds. A tasteless and odourless liquid at room temperature, it has the important ability to dissolve many other substances. Indeed, the versatility of water as a solvent is essential to living organisms.
